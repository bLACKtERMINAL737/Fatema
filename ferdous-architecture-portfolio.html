About

Every material has a memory. I try to design in a way that lets it keep telling its story.

I'm Jannatul Ferdous Fatema, an early-stage architecture student who believes design begins long before a building does — with a line, a point, a shadow. My first year has been spent learning to reduce the everyday to its essential geometry, and then to rebuild it with intention.

My process moves between the analytical and the intuitive: reading a site the way you'd read a line drawing, then testing an idea by hand — with paper, buttons, foam, colour — before it ever reaches a screen. I'm most interested in the space between structure and feeling, where function has to earn its form.

Currently completing Level 1, Term 1 of my B.Arch, building a foundation in composition, colour theory, spatial relief, and dimensional thinking — the vocabulary every architect needs before they can speak in buildings.
